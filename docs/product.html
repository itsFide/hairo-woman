3
Additional causes of
hair health issues:
Genetics is a key factor in hair loss, particularly in patterns like male and female pattern baldness. If your family has a history of hair loss, you carry genes that predispose you to this condition. This genetic influence can determine not only the likelihood of hair loss but also the age at which it might begin and its progression pattern.
Regular heat styling tools expose your hair to high temperatures, which can weaken hair proteins and cause structural damage. Over time, this leads to brittle hair, breakage, and thinning. Excessive heat can also damage your scalp, affecting healthy hair growth.
Chronic stress can impact your body in various ways, including your hair. It can trigger telogen effluvium, a condition where a large number of hair follicles enter the resting phase, leading to increased shedding. Stress can also exacerbate scalp conditions like dandruff or dermatitis, indirectly affecting hair health.
